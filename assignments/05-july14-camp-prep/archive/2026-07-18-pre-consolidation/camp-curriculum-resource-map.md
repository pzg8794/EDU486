## EQUITAS Adaptation Check

Before reusing an activity, ask:

- Does the activity let youth influence the question, explanation, or public action?
- Does it distinguish an access barrier from a thinking barrier?
- Can learners participate through different roles, energy levels, sensory conditions, languages, and communication modes?
- Does it locate plastic pollution in systems of production, policy, labor, consumption, and unequal exposure rather than blaming individual families?
- Does technology reveal evidence and expand agency, or add avoidable reading, memory, interface, surveillance, or stamina demands?
- Is joy part of rigorous collaboration, creativity, and belonging rather than a reward after the science?

## Evidence Arc To Borrow

1. Notice a local phenomenon and make an initial model.
2. Build a shared question board and choose a question the group needs to answer.
3. Collect or examine local evidence with accessible roles and safety alternatives.
4. Analyze patterns and limits: what can this evidence support, and what remains invisible?
5. Revise the model using youth observations, community knowledge, and scientific sources.
6. Create art or communication that advocates for a more just future.
7. Ask a real audience to respond, decide, or act.
