## Read This First

Backward design begins with the understanding and transfer learners should achieve, then determines acceptable evidence, and only then chooses instruction, activities, technologies, and materials. This protects a unit from the twin failures of fun activity without intellectual purpose and content coverage without meaning. Through the Puzzle Plan, Stage 2 becomes especially important: evidence must reveal scientific thinking without mistaking access friction for inability.

## What The Source Actually Contributes

- **Stage 1 - desired results:** identify what youth should know, understand, and be able to do, then prioritize enduring ideas because available time is limited (pp. 17-18).
- **Stage 2 - acceptable evidence:** decide what would demonstrate understanding before planning lessons; use collected evidence across formal and informal assessments, not only a final test (pp. 18-19).
- **Stage 3 - learning plan:** select knowledge, skills, coaching, activities, sequence, and resources that prepare learners to produce the desired evidence (pp. 18-19).
- **Twin sins:** activity-oriented design can become "hands-on without being minds-on," while coverage can move through facts without an overarching purpose (pp. 15-16).
- **Transparency:** students should be able to explain what they are doing, why they are doing it, how it connects, and how they will demonstrate learning (p. 16).

> "The best designs derive backward from the learnings sought" (p. 14).

## Puzzle Plan / EQUITAS Interpretation

**Access:** Backward design lets us separate the non-negotiable intellectual goal from accidental demands in a particular activity. If the goal is causal explanation from evidence, a long handwritten paragraph, rapid speech, or a complex app may be unnecessary barriers.

**Identity and healing:** Desired results should not require youth to disclose trauma, diagnosis, culture, health, or family circumstances. Understanding can be connected to identity and community without making private experience the price of participation.

**Agency:** Stage 2 can include meaningful choice in how evidence is created, but choice is not a collection of unrelated tasks. Each route must reveal the same core understanding and allow youth to explain or defend their reasoning.

**Criticality:** "Acceptable evidence" is a diagnostic decision. EQUITAS asks whose communication is legible to the rubric, what context the measure excludes, and whether tool fluency, stamina, or English dominance is being scored as scientific understanding.

**Transformation:** The end goal should include transfer: youth use the explanation to evaluate a claim, make a design decision, communicate with a community, or advocate for change.

## Invisible Invaders Backward Design

**Stage 1 - transfer goal:** Youth will use a causal model and sample evidence to explain how suspected microplastics can reach indoor and outdoor environments, state what their evidence can and cannot prove, and propose a justice-centered response.

**Stage 2 - acceptable evidence:** A youth can demonstrate understanding through a revised model plus an oral, written, visual, audio, or partner-supported explanation. Evidence must include source, transport, sample comparison, uncertainty, and a system-level action; presentation polish is scored separately, if at all.

**Stage 3 - learning plan:** Start with the phenomenon, elicit questions and initial models, practice contamination-aware observation, compare parking-lot/classroom/blank evidence, consult direct sources, revise models, examine unequal burdens and decision-making power, and create advocacy for a real audience.

## Tension To Carry Into Tuesday

Backward design can become overly teacher-controlled if the endpoint is so fixed that youth questions cannot change the route or deepen the goal. Storylines and JuST press against that risk by sharing epistemic agency. My design challenge is to hold a rigorous transfer goal while letting youth knowledge, questions, access needs, and evidence genuinely reshape how the class gets there.
