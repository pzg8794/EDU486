## Tool 3 - Justice And Power Map

Choose one pathway or plastic-related system. Evidence may come from samples, maps, observations, community knowledge, or direct sources.

For each prompt, record **our current evidence or idea** and **what we still need**.

- Who produces or sells it?
- Who benefits from the current system?
- Who uses it, and what choices are realistically available?
- Who may face exposure or environmental burden?
- Who performs cleanup, maintenance, or monitoring labor?
- Who can make or block a change?
- Whose knowledge is treated as credible?
- Who or what more-than-human life is affected?

**Justice check:** Shared responsibility does not mean equal responsibility, equal power, equal benefit, or equal access to alternatives.

## Tool 6 - Showcase And Audience Feedback

### Shared Scientific Criteria

- The explanation includes a plastic-containing material, release mechanism, movement through connected systems, possible exposure route, human-sample evidence, and evidence limit.
- The product distinguishes detection from prevalence, route, and health effect.
- The action request fits the available evidence.
- The justice analysis considers benefit, burden, labor, alternatives, and decision power.
- The revised model shows what changed and why.

### Production Choice

Artistic polish, speaking speed, accent, handwriting, reading speed, technology expertise, eye contact, standing tolerance, and public performance are not substitutes for scientific reasoning.

### Audience Feedback

Audience members may respond by sticky note, form, drawing, audio, conversation, or anonymous message.

- I understand...
- I am still wondering...
- The evidence that affected my thinking was...
- A possible action or commitment is...
- A concern or unintended consequence is...
