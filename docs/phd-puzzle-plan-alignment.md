# EDU486 Alignment With PhD and Puzzle Plan Goals

## Why EDU486 Matters Strategically

EDU486 is not just another technology integration course. It is a field-facing implementation space where Piter can show that his broader work on equitable diagnostics, inclusive AI, and digitally rich teaching can become usable, justice-centered learning for youth.

The course creates a bridge between:

- PhD preparation in equitable diagnostics and inclusive AI
- Warner teacher preparation in Computer Science K-12 and Digitally-Rich Teaching
- community-based STEM teaching through Freedom School / Planet Protectors Camp
- accessible pedagogy for neurodivergent, CLD, multilingual, and marginalized learners

## Core Puzzle Plan Connection

The Drive planning materials emphasize a unified research ecosystem: technical expertise, equity frameworks, teaching innovation, and portfolio coherence should reinforce each other instead of living as separate course products.

EDU486 can contribute to that ecosystem through:

1. **Technical translation.** Convert complex data/AI thinking into youth-accessible environmental investigation.
2. **Community evidence.** Treat local knowledge and youth questions as legitimate data sources, not side notes.
3. **Inclusive design.** Use UDL, multilingual supports, clear roles, flexible outputs, and reduced unnecessary cognitive load.
4. **Justice-centered technology.** Ask whether a tool expands agency, voice, and action rather than only increasing efficiency.
5. **Portfolio proof.** Produce public artifacts that show a coherent teaching/research identity.

## Proposed Course Research Question

> How can accessible digital tools help middle school youth investigate environmental justice questions while keeping community knowledge, cultural wealth, and student agency at the center?

## Personal Teaching Identity Link

This course should show Piter as a teacher-scholar who can:

- design STEM learning that treats students as capable knowledge-makers
- use technology without letting technology erase place, culture, language, or bodymind access
- connect community environmental action to computational thinking and evidence-making
- make science learning emotionally and intellectually safe for students whose ways of knowing have often been underestimated
