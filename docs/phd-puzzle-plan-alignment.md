# EDU486 Alignment With PhD and Puzzle Plan Goals

## Why EDU486 Matters Strategically

EDU486 is not just another technology integration course. It is a field-facing implementation space where Piter can show that his broader work on equitable diagnostics, inclusive AI, disability justice, chronic illness access, and digitally rich teaching can become usable, justice-centered learning for youth.

The course creates a bridge between:

- PhD preparation in equitable diagnostics, inclusive AI, diagnostic justice, and health/education equity
- Warner teacher preparation in Computer Science K-12 and Digitally-Rich Teaching
- community-based STEM teaching through Freedom School / Planet Protectors Camp
- accessible pedagogy for neurodivergent, chronically ill, disabled, CLD, multilingual, and multiply marginalized learners

## Core Puzzle Plan Connection

The [Drive planning materials](artifact-index.md#prior-work-and-drive-sources) emphasize a unified research ecosystem: technical expertise, equity frameworks, teaching innovation, lived experience, and portfolio coherence should reinforce each other instead of living as separate course products.

EDU486 can contribute to that ecosystem through:

1. **Technical translation.** Convert complex data/AI thinking into youth-accessible environmental investigation without erasing disability, language, energy, or sensory access.
2. **Community evidence.** Treat local knowledge, youth questions, family knowledge, bodymind experience, and disability culture as legitimate evidence, not side notes.
3. **Inclusive design.** Use UDL, SIOP, multilingual supports, clear roles, low-energy pathways, flexible outputs, and reduced unnecessary cognitive load.
4. **Justice-centered technology.** Ask whether a tool expands agency, voice, access, and critique rather than only increasing efficiency.
5. **Portfolio proof.** Produce public artifacts that show a coherent teaching/research identity grounded in Access, Identity, Healing, Agency, Criticality, Joy, and Transformation.

## Proposed Course Research Question

> How can accessible digital tools help neurodivergent, chronically ill, disabled, CLD, multilingual, and multiply marginalized middle school youth investigate environmental justice questions while keeping bodymind access, community knowledge, cultural wealth, and student agency at the center?

## Personal Teaching Identity Link

This course should show Piter as a teacher-scholar who can:

- design STEM learning that treats students as capable knowledge-makers whose thinking may appear through multiple modes
- use technology without letting technology erase place, culture, language, or bodymind access
- connect community environmental action to computational thinking and evidence-making
- make science learning emotionally, sensory-wise, linguistically, medically, and intellectually safe for students whose ways of knowing have often been underestimated
